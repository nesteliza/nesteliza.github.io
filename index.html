I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the Technion, advised by Shay Moran and Yuval Filmus.

Research

Broadly, I am interested in the interaction between geometry, privacy, and learning theory. My work studies “reconstruction games,” where a researcher queries noisy information about a hidden object (for example, distance or linear queries), while an analyst tries to protect sensitive information.

Previously, I worked in algebraic geometry. During my undergraduate studies, I worked with Sergey Gorchinskiy on topics involving unipotent modules and homological methods. Later, in my master’s studies, I worked with Howard Nuer on problems in birational geometry and Bridgeland stability of moduli spaces.
